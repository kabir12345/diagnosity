1. Specimen arrival & dissection

After the patient undergoes a gall bladder surgery(say), her gall bladder tissue arrives at the path lab for examination The tissue is then gross examined by disection by a histo pathologist, who determines sections of interest in that specimen

3. Slide preparation

The micron layer of the specimen is then covered by a special stain that enables the pathologist to see various parts of cells/cells under the microscope. Gives colour basically

2. Processing & Embedding

The section of interest is put in a tube/small container and then goes for processing. Processing means it is cut into very very thin layers( 5 microns… 5 thousands of a millimeter that is) and put on a glass slide

Embedding means taking rest of the tissue and putting it into a wax block for preservation and to enable us to take more micron sections in the future

4. Diagnosis

The pathologist examines the slide and then gives out the diagnosis
